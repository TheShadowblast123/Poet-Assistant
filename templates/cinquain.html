What is a cinquain?

Developed by American poet Adelaide Crapsey, the Cinquain offers a concise yet expressive style. The Cinquain's structured syllable and stress count ([2, 1], [4, 2], [6, 3], [4, 8], [2, 1]) provides a balanced rhythm and allows for a focused expression of an idea or emotion. Despite its brevity, a well-crafted Cinquain can evoke vivid imagery and convey profound feelings, making it an intriguing form for poets to explore creative expression within a limited space.

So how does this help?

In order to keep the poet honest, this website provides live syllable counting. You may also choose to change words to their synonym if that would be a better fit!
