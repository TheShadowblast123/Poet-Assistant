What is a sonnet?

A Sonnet is a poetic form that originated in Italy and has become a popular form of expression in English literature. It traditionally consists of 14 lines and follows specific rhyme schemes and metrical patterns. There are two main types of Sonnets English/Shakespearean and Italian/Petrarchan. Sonnets are known for their elegance, rhythm, and poetic craft. They offer a structured framework for poets to explore various themes, emotions, and ideas.
