What is a villanelle?

A Villanelle is a highly structured and intricate form of poetry that consists of 19 lines. It originated from the Italian pastoral song and was popularized in English literature during the 19th and 30th centuries. Villanelles often explore complex emotions, existential questions, or meditations on time, memory, and change. The strict structure challenges poets to craft each line with precision and to create a cohesive and powerful whole.
